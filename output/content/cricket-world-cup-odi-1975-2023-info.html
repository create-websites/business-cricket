Who Owns the Crown?

Since 1975, only six nations have lifted the coveted trophy. The chart below shows the distribution of titles, highlighting an era of dominance by one particular powerhouse.

Australia: The Juggernaut

With 6 titles, Australia has won more World Cups than any other nation, including a historic treble from 1999 to 2007.

The Two-Time Winners

The West Indies (1975, 1979) and India (1983, 2011) are the only other nations to have won the tournament more than once.

A Timeline of Triumph

From the traditional 60-over format in whites to the modern 50-over spectacle, the World Cup has seen distinct eras of dominance. This timeline tracks the champions through the years.

WI

1975 & 1979: The Caribbean Conquest

The West Indies, led by Clive Lloyd, established a dynasty with back-to-back wins, defining the early years with fearsome pace and powerful batting.

IN

1983: The Upset of the Century

India, as 66-1 underdogs, defeated the mighty West Indies, an event that shifted cricket's center of gravity eastward forever.

AU

1987 & 1999-2007: The Australian Era

Australia's journey began in 1987, but their golden era was a ruthless run of three consecutive titles, establishing one of sport's great dynasties.

PK/SL

1992 & 1996: Subcontinent Revolution

Pakistan's "cornered tigers" won in 1992, followed by Sri Lanka's tactical revolution in 1996, showcasing the subcontinent's rising power.

IN/AU/EN

2011-2019: The Home Advantage

A new trend emerged as India (2011), Australia (2015), and England (2019) all lifted the trophy on home soil, breaking a long-standing jinx.

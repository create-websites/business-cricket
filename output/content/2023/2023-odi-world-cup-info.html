Clash of Titans: The Knockouts

India produced a performance of overwhelming dominance, posting a record 397/4. Virat Kohli's historic 50th ODI ton and Mohammed Shami's 7-wicket haul sealed a 70-run win.

Autopsy of the Final: The Ahmedabad Silence

The final was a tactical masterclass from Australia. Their decision to field first on a slow pitch, backed by exceptional fielding, neutralized India's explosive batting. The Masterstroke: The Toss

Pat Cummins' decision to bowl first was a calculated gamble. He correctly predicted the pitch would be difficult to bat on initially but would ease up under lights with the evening dew, neutralizing India's spin threat and giving his batters the advantage.

The Turning Point: The Catch

With India flying at 76/1, Travis Head took a spectacular diving catch to dismiss the dangerous Rohit Sharma. This moment shifted the momentum entirely, leading to a "boundary drought" of over 90 balls and preventing India from posting a commanding total.
